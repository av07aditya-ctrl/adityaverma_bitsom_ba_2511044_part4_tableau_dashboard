## Executive Summary

Our business generated **₹21.7Cr** in total sales and **₹3.3Cr** in profit at an overall margin of **15.35%** over a 24-month period. The trend is positive — sales are growing, customer volumes are expanding, and key regional markets are strengthening. However, three strategic threats require immediate attention: excessive discounting that erodes margins, a 4.55% return rate concentrated in specific categories, and uneven channel attribution that limits our ability to optimise marketing ROI.

---

## Risks Visible in the Data 🚨

1. **Margin Erosion via Discounting:** The scatter chart of Discount vs. Profit clearly shows a negative correlation. Orders at 35% discount have an average profit margin of approximately 4.9%. Without a discount governance policy, this will worsen.

2. **Regional Concentration Risk:** South region contributes disproportionately to total revenue. A regional disruption (logistics, competition, regulatory) could significantly impact company-level results.

3. **Return Rate in High-Value Categories:** Returns in Furniture and specific Technology sub-categories represent both revenue reversals and brand reputation risk. At 4.55% overall, we are within norms, but the trajectory must be watched.

4. **Channel Attribution Gap:** 0.6% of orders have no recorded campaign source. This prevents accurate ROI calculation and makes budget allocation decisions unreliable.

---

## Opportunities Visible in the Data 🌱

1. **Home Office Segment Expansion:** Currently the smallest segment (1,446 orders), this represents untapped potential given post-pandemic work-from-home trends.

2. **Technology Category Investment:** Highest margin category — shifting product mix toward Technology through targeted promotions could improve overall portfolio margin by 2-3 percentage points.

3. **East Region Growth:** With dedicated investment, East could close the gap to South. Infrastructure and brand-building investments here have high asymmetric upside.

4. **Referral Channel Scale:** Referral is high-intent, low-cost. Building a formal referral program (incentivised) could significantly expand this channel at a low customer acquisition cost.

## Limitations of This Dashboard

1. **No LTV Data:** We see 30-day or transaction-level revenue but cannot compute Lifetime Value — this limits customer segment strategy
2. **No Cost of Acquisition:** Campaign channel data shows sales but not the cost of each channel — true ROI is uncomputable
3. **24 unattributed orders** — biases channel analysis
4. **No competitor data** — regional performance context is missing without market share benchmarks
5. **2-year window** — may not be sufficient to identify multi-year cyclical patterns
